🚗 LA Parking & Curb Laws

Curb Color Guide
Red Curb
No stopping, standing, or parking at any time. Fire lanes, hydrant zones, and bus zones are typically marked red.
Yellow Curb
Commercial loading zone. Drivers of non-commercial vehicles may stop briefly to load/unload passengers. Typically limited to 20–30 minutes during posted hours (usually 7 am – 6 pm).
White Curb
Passenger loading only — 5-minute limit. For picking up or dropping off people. No loading of freight or merchandise.
Green Curb
Short-term parking — time limit posted on sign or curb (commonly 10–30 minutes). Enforced during posted hours.
Blue Curb
Reserved for persons with disabilities (ADA). A valid Disabled Person placard or license plate is required. Available 24 hours a day.
Common LA Parking Rules
🧹
Street Sweeping: Check posted signs — fines are $73+. Days and times vary by block. Sweeping days are Mon–Fri in most neighborhoods.
🕐
Posted Time Limits: Exceeding the posted limit (1 hr, 2 hr, 4 hr) is enforced during sign hours, typically 8 am – 6 pm Mon–Sat. Moving the car one block does not reset the limit.
🏘️
Preferential Parking Districts (PPD): Residents with permits can park; non-permit vehicles limited to 2 hours or as posted. Over 80 districts citywide.
🔥
Fire Hydrants: No parking within 15 feet of a fire hydrant, even without a red curb marking. Fine is $80+.
✂️
Crosswalks & Intersections: No parking within 20 feet of a crosswalk or intersection. No parking within 30 feet of a stop sign or signal.
🚌
Bus Stops: No stopping in a bus zone at any time. Red curb is usually painted — but the restriction applies regardless of curb color if a bus stop sign is present.
⛽
Driveways: No parking in front of a public or private driveway — even your own — in a way that blocks access.
🌙
Overnight Oversized Vehicles: Vehicles over 22 ft long or 7 ft tall may not park on a residential street between 2 am – 6 am without a permit.
📅
72-Hour Rule: A vehicle cannot be parked on the same public street for more than 72 consecutive hours. Violations may result in a citation or tow.
This is a general reference only. Always check posted signs — they override curb colors.
